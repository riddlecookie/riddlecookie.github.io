I woke up at 6 am in the morning. "It's today!" I thought. "Time to finally move this lazy ass!". Jumped out of the bed. Brushed my teeth. Searched the closet searching for a clean T-shirt. It's the laundry day... Took a dirty T-shirt out of the bin. It smelled bad... "Nothing will stop me!". I put on some old shoes and go out of the house. It's raining. "Well, Rocky didn't give up that easily! I can be like Rocky!".

After a little stretching I start running. Foo Fighters in my headphones. It's not that bad.

After some time my body start screaming "hey buddy! What's going on?! What are we doing?!". I'm ingoring this voice. I run further.

Some more time passes on, and I'm totally exhausted. "Rambo would never give up!" I tell myself. "Just a little more!". And I'm still running.

"Just 200 more meters! 100! 50! 10! That's it! I made it!" I tell myself after pretty impressive distance. I am really proud of myself. With a smile on my face I wait for an elevator, because I'm too tired to climb the stairs to my apartment. I take a shower, prepare some food and just go back to my daily routine.

The next thay I wake up at 7 am. Missed a couple of alarms. "Oh shit! I overslept! But that's fine, if I hurry up, I can still complete my run before going to work.". I'm getting up from the bed. "Oh, oh..." I think while falling on my face. An image of myself as Sylvester Stallone in my head changes a little bit from the one, where I'm at the top of the stairs in Philadelphia with my fists up in the air into something more like

# It's all about the volume

Let's begin with a really great clip from the *Joe Rogan Experience* podcast (it's from the episode *JRE MMA Show \#32 with Firas Zahabi*):

<div style="text-align: center;"><iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/_fbCcWyYthQ" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture" allowfullscreen></iframe></div>

I think, that points made by Zahabi can be applied not only to sport, but actually to any kind of activity. For me, the three most important ones are:
1. **Aim for the maximal volume** (that is, think of yourself as a marathoner rather than sprinter).
2. Remembering the first, **there will come times of intensified effort**.
3. Try to keep yourself in the **flow** state.

Let's say, that you have a job, in which you you can manage your time at least in the minimal scope. According to Zahabi, you should spread your tasks in a reasonable way between five working days, instead of overworking yourself for three days and then dying for the remaining two. Seems obivious? It is. But I really like such slogans, that I can keep in my mind and stick to, and I hope they will also be of some use to you too :)

What about the second point? Well, this is just as simple. Something unpredictable will pop up from time to time and you'll have to deal with it. Or some deadline will be approaching and you'll have to finish a couple of projects at once to satisfy the client.

[...]
Or you would just be in the **flow**, which you shouldn't stop! Why restrain yourself, if you are motivated and just enjoy the work you do? You should aim for this state!
